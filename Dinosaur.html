One of my first questions I wanted to know was about the name. Dinosaur.
Where does that come from? How do you come up with that name? Dinosaur!
It certainly conjures up only one thing where you read it! Turns out, it should a bit silly. "Terrible Lizard."
Online Etymology Dictionary is a great resource for word nerds like me. From there I learned;

According to the Online Etymology Dictionary, the word is made up from two parts. Dino (Latin) from Dire which possibly comes from ancient Oscan and Umbrian and perhaps Greek for deinos meaning "terrible."
Of course there's much more to it than that but I'll leave you to the searching and reading further.

The last part -saurus is the Latinized form of a Greek word meaning Lizard.
Thus we have "Terrible Lizard."
Personally, I like Dinosaur better.
